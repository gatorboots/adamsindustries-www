A house in South Louisiana has to survive three things a house in Ohio doesn't: 140-mph gusts, four feet of standing water, and a humidity level that will find any gap you missed in the envelope. Pretending otherwise is how you end up with the work torn out a year in. Adams Industries builds residential like the next storm is already forming, because around here it usually is.

## What We Handle

- **New construction and additions.** Ground-up work with a licensed supervisor you can reach directly.
- **Post-storm rebuilds.** Structural, roofing, envelope, interiors. We write the estimate to match the way your carrier reads documentation.
- **Water damage.** Extraction, drying, mold remediation, and reconstruction — not just cosmetic repair that hides it.
- **Roofing.** Replacement, repair, weatherproofing. Impact-rated where it matters.
- **Electrical, plumbing, HVAC.** State-licensed trades on our jobs. No "handyman" rewiring.
- **Kitchens, baths, finish.** The stuff you actually see. Scheduled so your family isn't camping in the garage for nine months.

## Working With Your Insurance

Insurance is where most residential projects go sideways. We write estimates in the language carriers use, photograph and document damage the way adjusters want it documented, and handle the back-and-forth so you aren't stuck on the phone explaining what a "purlin" is to somebody three states away.

## Parish-Correct Permitting

Louisiana's parishes don't all read the code the same way. What flies in East Baton Rouge gets a correction notice in St. Tammany. Our supervisors know the quirks of the parishes we work in, and we pull permits correctly the first time — which saves you the two-week delay from a bounced inspection.

## How a Residential Project Runs

1. Emergency stabilization and damage documentation (if you're coming to us post-storm)
2. Detailed assessment and a written estimate you can put in front of anybody
3. Permit acquisition and pre-construction review
4. Construction with regular progress checks and photo updates
5. Final walkthrough and sign-off — we stay reachable after the sign-off, too
