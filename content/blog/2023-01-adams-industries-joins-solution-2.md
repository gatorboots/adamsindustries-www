We're glad to confirm what many of you already knew informally: **Adams Industries is a participating contractor for Solution 2 of the Restore Louisiana Homeowner Assistance Program**.

This isn't a new thing we're doing. It's the paperwork finally catching up to the work we've been doing for two decades — licensed Louisiana residential construction with documented scopes, insurance coordination, and a schedule that actually holds.

## What this means if you're a homeowner

If your home qualifies for the Restore Louisiana Homeowner Assistance Program and you've chosen Solution 2 (or are still deciding), you can pick Adams Industries directly.

Specifically:

- We're a Louisiana-licensed general contractor, verifiable through the [Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors](https://www.lslbc.louisiana.gov/).
- We carry general liability and workers' compensation insurance; certificates are on file and available on request.
- We maintain an A+ rating with the Better Business Bureau.
- We've done residential work — new construction, additions, and post-storm rebuilds — across Louisiana for over twenty years.

The program will verify our credentials the same way we publish them. That's how Solution 2 is designed to work.

## What we commit to

For every Solution 2 project we take on:

- Free initial site walk and review of your ECR against the actual damage
- Written Project Plan with a scope tied line-by-line to the ECR
- Five-draw schedule structured so payment tracks actual completion, not front-loaded
- No advance payments (the program doesn't allow them and we wouldn't ask)
- Written change orders for anything outside the original scope, with program pre-approval where required
- Regular progress documentation you can actually see
- Final walk-through with you before sign-off
- A phone number that still works after the project closes

## How to get started

If you have an award letter or ECR in hand, call us at [(985) 255-2435](tel:+19852552435). We'll set a time to walk the property, review the ECR against current site conditions, and draft a Project Plan for your review.

If you don't have an award letter yet — if you've filed the survey but the application is still moving through the program — we can still help you think about what a Project Plan would look like, so when your grant is executed you're ready to move.
